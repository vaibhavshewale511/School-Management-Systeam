Leading Nursery School In Tokyo

Welcome to our Nursery School in Tokyo, where we specialize in providing an innovative Montessori environment designed for optimal physical, emotional, and intellectual development. Our commitment to excellence is reflected in our world-class proprietary Global Montessori Plus programme

Award-Winning GMP Curriculum for Nursery And Kindergarten

GIIS’ Montessori education focuses on our students' formative years. Our innovative Montessori pedagogy is combined with cutting-edge teaching methods to provide young learners with opportunities to excel in their areas of interest. Our Global Montessori Plus (GMP) programme stands for a distinctive and well-designed nursery school curriculum.

Our curriculum also aids in moulding students’ skills, abilities, and personalities, making GIIS Tokyo Montessori school the most trusted Japanese kindergarten experience. We nurture students in a joyful and safe atmosphere, preparing them for primary education and life beyond the classroom. As a pioneer Tokyo nursery school in Japan, we have designed our award-winning GMP curriculum with the finest of traditional montessori education combined with digital led teaching methodologies.

A Glimpse of Preschool Education in Japan

The two forms of Early childhood education are Day care and Kindergartens. Preschool or Early childhood education is not considered mandatory in Japan, therefore Kindergartens are run privately like GIIS and the ideal age to enrol in Japanese Kindergarten is between three to five. The rudimentary direction indicated by the Ministry of Education - Japan towards the Kindergarten learning framework is Culture, Technology, Science and Sports.
‍
Japanese Kindergartens also promote and instill in students -  respect for family, school and teachers. Young Kindergartners at GIIS are taught to develop healthy minds and bodies through play and in group-oriented environments.

What is the Plus?

GMP's unique value offering is its adaptation of internationally acclaimed Montessori teaching methodology with an added advantage of a ‘plus’. It combines traditional Montessori methods with modern teaching and learning tools enriched with cutting-edge technology to promote children's holistic growth and development. This award-winning curriculum prepares students for further education and challenges of the future.

Going Green

We encourage our students to achieve excellence in academics, while also learning to care for nature and the environment around us. Our environmental programmes, such as Go Green, emphasise over the necessity of recycling and upcycling, as well as how to efficiently use them to decrease waste. We organise various environmental drives, such as planting of trees, sowing seeds, celebration of festivals and birthdays in an eco-friendly manner by making the best out of classroom waste. This encourages our students to actively participate in environmental awareness programmes.

Community Partnerships

GIIS Tokyo has earned the title of finest nursery school in Japan thanks to its outstanding educational programme that encourages students to engage in community service and to be morally and socially responsible. Our school offers fantastic opportunities to students to participate in events, such as fundraisers and book collection drives, as well as environmental drives that will help them learn how they can make the world a better place to live.

What is the significance of preschool education?

Early childhood is a critical period in a child's development. During these years, students require a holistic approach to learn in order to achieve their full potential. Preschool education is critical for a student's emotional, social, and physical development.

What are the different curricula offered by GIIS Tokyo?

As one of the top-notch Indian schools in Japan, GIIS offers some of the best International curriculums:
‍
1.  Global Montessori Programme (GMP)
2. Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE)
3. International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme (IB DP)
4. International Baccalaureate Primary Years Programme (IB PYP)
5. Cambridge Lower Secondary Programme (CLSP)
6. Cambridge International General Certificate Secondary Programme (IGCSE)

What are the main pillars of the Global Montessori Plus Programme?

The GMP is built on five pillars:
‍
● Excelerate Program
● Multi-faceted Learning
● iCare Program
● iPlay Program
● Future Ready Program

How are the students assessed in Montessori grades?

Weekly and term-based examinations are used to track the progress of the students. Parents are kept up to date on their child's progress through direct feedback, parent-teacher conferences, MyGIIS Parent Portal, and meet-and-greet sessions.

How many campuses does GIIS have in Tokyo?

We have three world-class campuses across Tokyo:
‍
● Nishi Kasai Campus
● Higashi Kasai Campus
● Seishincho Campus

What is the Montessori curriculum followed at GIIS preschool?

GIIS is known for its world-class kindergarten education in Japan. GIIS’ Global Montessori Plus Program is an award-winning curriculum that combines the best of Montessori's principles with current teaching enriched with innovative technologies for great holistic student growth.

What is the age criteria to enrol in the preschool programme?

 Our GIIS preschool Tokyo campus caters to nursery and kindergarten grades. The following is the age criteria:

● Nursery entry at Age 3+ as of 31st May 
● Kindergarten 1 entry at Age 4+ as of 31st May
● Kindergarten 2 entry at Age 5+ as of 31st May

Will a student be able to cope up with the syllabus if he/she begins in the middle of the year?

As one of the best nursery schools, we make sure all our students are provided with expert assistance and peer support so that they feel comfortable no matter when they join. Our unique Bridge Programme helps students cope up with academics as well as allows them to smoothly form new friendships with existing students.

What is the annual fee for the preschool programme?

The Montessori and Kindergarten fees in Tokyo are based on the grade and academic year. Please visit GIIS’ Tokyo fees page for details.

Are there any late payment fee charges?

Yes, if the fee is not submitted before the due date, JPY 100 per day will be charged as late fee payment charges.

What are the different modes of payment acceptable at GIIS?

GIIS accepts several convenient modes of payment like cheques, cash, debit cards, credit cards (all available at the school campus). We also accept online payments through MyGIIS Parent Portal.

Is a sibling fee discount available at GIIS?

Parents enrolling more than one child at GIIS Tokyo are eligible for 25% tuition and school bus fees discount for the second child. A 50% discount on tuition fees can be availed in case of the third child’s enrolment.

What is the procedure to cancel the transportation service?

For the cancellation of bus services, parents are required to submit a one-month notice to the school. In case the notice is not submitted or less than a month is given for the notice, full payment in lieu of notice will be charged. The cancellation request can be made through the MyGIIS Parent Portal'.

Does GIIS offer a campus tour?

Parents and students can take advantage of GIIS's unique campus tour facilities. To visit the campus, you must first register in order to use the facility during the designated times.
